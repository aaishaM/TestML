Machine learning has become integral in powering various aspects of our modern technological landscape. However, the rise of sophisticated adversaries poses a significant threat to the integrity of these models. Adversarial attacks, carefully crafted perturbations that can deceive machine learning systems, can compromise the reliability and trustworthiness of these models, leading to potentially catastrophic consequences. To safeguard the robustness and security of machine learning models, our testing tool serves as a crucial defense mechanism, meticulously assessing the resilience of these models against a spectrum of adversarial attacks. By providing comprehensive and dynamic testing capabilities, we empower organizations to fortify their AI systems, ensuring their effectiveness and trustworthiness in the face of evolving threats, thereby fostering a secure and resilient AI ecosystem for the future.

LITERATURE SURVEY

Studies in the field of machine learning attacks have primarily focused on various types of attacks, including evasion attacks, backdoor attacks, and model theft. Evasion attacks involve introducing perturbations to input data during test time, causing models to misclassify and fail. These attacks can be categorized into black box and white box attacks, with research extending to specific applications such as biometric authentication and malware detection systems. Defense mechanisms against evasion attacks include adversarial training and input data regularization. In response to the vulnerabilities of deep neural networks, researchers have proposed various techniques such as first-order techniques within the saddle point formulation and the use of the Projected Gradient Descent (PGD) algorithm for generating strong adversarial attacks

The study of machine learning attacks has investigated a range of techniques, including the Projected Gradient Descent (PGD) algorithm, which systematically manipulates input data to produce strong adversarial attacks, revealing the fragility of deep learning systems. The Boundary Attack, on the other hand, moves through the space between adversarial and non-adversarial examples, reducing the difference between the two through iterative rejection sampling. Furthermore, the Carlini and Wagner attack has been used across different domains, utilizing various distance metrics such as L0, L2, and L∞ to generate potent adversarial examples. These attacks underscore the pressing need for robust defense mechanisms, prompting researchers to explore strategies such as adversarial training, regularization of input data, and the development of novel paradigms like the Boundary-Optimized Shotz-Bosh (BOSH) algorithm. Additionally, the integration of counterfactual explanations and the use of auto-encoders with noise injection have emerged as promising methods to fortify machine learning models against adversarial attacks.

Furthermore, studies have introduced novel concepts like the "Adversarial Mask," which utilizes gradient-based optimization to create imperceptible adversarial masks capable of fooling facial recognition systems. The Carlini and Wagner attack, employing various distance metrics, has been utilized for attacking neural network models in both image and audio domains, highlighting the need for robust defense mechanisms. Additionally, the development of techniques like Boundary Attack and the Boundary-Optimized Shotz-Bosh (BOSH) algorithm has contributed to the advancement of Decision-Based Attacks (DBAs). Furthermore, research has explored defense strategies such as counterfactual explanations and the use of auto-encoders with noise injection to enhance model resilience against adversarial attacks.

METHODOLOGY

The research unfolds in several stages. Initially, a churn prediction model is constructed utilizing a telecom churn dataset. To address data imbalance and prevent overfitting, SMOTE and random under-sampling techniques are employed. The development of this churn prediction model is carried out using a Convolutional Neural Network.

Following that, three distinct attack strategies are devised:

  1. Projected Gradient Descent
  2. Boundary Attack
  3. Carlini and Wagner.


These are three attacks belonging to the category of Evasion. These attacks are leveraged to create adversarial examples that introduce distortions, subsequently causing misclassification by the model. These attacks are applied during the training phase of the machine learning model lifecycle. The research closely monitors the changes in model accuracy resulting from these attacks to ensure whether the model is vulnerable.

In the final phase, a testing system is crafted, allowing users to upload their own models and datasets. They can then select and apply a specific attack, while closely observing the ensuing accuracy changes. In cases where the model is found to be vulnerable, the system will provide recommendations for implementing defense mechanisms to bolster the model's resilience against these attacks.

Recommendations are provided according to the attack the model is vulnerable to,
  1. Project Gradient Decent- Adversarial Training, Stochastic Distillation
  2. Boundary Attack- Adversarial Training, Ensemble Method
  3. Carlini and Wagner- Adversarial Training, Defensive distillatio
RESEARCH PROBLEM

One of the most significant topics being researched at the moment is research on adversarial attacks. Adversarial attacks concentrate on slightly perturbing machine learning models in order to deceive the models into making false predictions. Since most sectors employ tabular data, attacking models trained on this form of data has garnered greater attention among these attacks on various model types and sectors. Additionally, as PGD, Carlini, and boundary attacks on tabular data are the three main attack types that have garnered concern in this period, they receive greater attention in accordance with our emphasis point, which will handle these three main attacks. PGD attack is a gradient based attack that iteratively perturbate the input data in multiple steps while constraining the perturbations within a specific range. The Boundary attack crosses decision boundaries in order to provide adversarial cases that result in misclassification. The Carlini attack uses an optimization-based methodology to produce the fewest possible model-fooling perturbations Investigating the 9 vulnerability of machine learning models trained on tabular data to these attacks and suggesting practical security strategies are the objectives of this research problem.

Identifying the hyper parameters of the aforementioned attack types and their respective performances on a tabular dataset trained model is one of the main focuses of this research. Through adjusting these hyperparameters and analyzing the resulting effects on our model, we may devise a strong defense mechanism or method to mitigate the model's vulnerability to such attacks.

This research will also focus on a particular sector-based robust model against adversarial attacks, which is another important issue. Since adversarial attacks affect different sectors in different ways, sector-based adversarial attack testing is also an important topic of research. Therefore, developing models that are resistant to these particular industries is essential. In conclusion, since churn is one of the most crucial models that are used in many organizations to retain customers, it is significant that no study has been done to forecast how sensitive are churn models to these assaults trained on tabular data. Therefore, if the model is fragile, a tiny quantity of disturbance might have a large-scale impact and result in a significant loss for the company

To conclude, the research challenge in this study is how to create a strong churn customer prediction model that is robust to boundary, PGD, and Carlini attacks that is trained using tabular data. Given that the current defense mechanisms and robust methodologies have only been evaluated on models trained on image datasets, it is unclear how they will perform when applied to a tabular dataset that differs from pictures in terms of features. As a result, finding potential robustness approaches for tabular data is important and receiving a lot of attention right now.

Main Objective

To asses the effect of various adversarial attacks on a churn prediction model and attempt to increase model strength against the given attacks.


Sub Objective 1

Sub Objective 2

RESEARCH GAP

Considering the existing researches with related to adversarial attacks most of the researchers are biased towards testing these attacks against models trained with image datasets. As mentioned in most of the existing researchers there is ample space to do more examination on how these adversarial attacks react to domain specific areas and how can we mitigate such attacks. As of now, no studies have been conducted on how these adversaries act in a customer churn specified domain and its related outcome. Thereby authors of this study closely examine how these adversarial attacks could be applied to structured data and then move into applying them in a churn prediction model to asses the vulnerability of those models built using a neural network since most of the current researchers are also focusing on building churn models using neural networks rather than a non-neural network. Accordingly, the central focus of this research is to apply PGD, C&W and adaptation of the Boundary Adversarial Attack specifically modified for tabular datasets on a churn prediction model which is a very sensitive business application that carries a lot of business value toan organization. It also emphasizes attacking models trained with tabular imbalanced data since it is a significantly understudied area. The proposed research will examine how the model’s accuracies will differ before and after applying these attacks and would intend to evaluate defense mechanisms against the proposed attack types to provide evident defenses that contributed to make the model robust against the tested attack types.
